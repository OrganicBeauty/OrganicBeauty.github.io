What To Do About Lack Of Elasticity

"The skin, recognized as the body's largest organ, comprises three primary layers: the outermost layer known as the epidermis, the middle layer referred to as the dermis, and the deep layer called the hypodermis. Within the middle layer, collagen and elastin proteins are synthesized, constituting the connective tissues responsible for the skin's elasticity. Consequently, it is these collagen and elastin fibers that facilitate the skin's ability to revert to its original position when stretched. It's important to note that various factors, including the aging process, contribute to a reduced production rate of these fibers and proteins. As a result, skin elasticity diminishes, leading to sagging and laxity in our skin."

What Causes Skin to Lose Its Elasticity?

"A decrease in skin elasticity can be attributed to a range of factors, but adhering to healthy habits and maintaining an overall well-balanced lifestyle can contribute to maximizing elasticity levels as we progress through the natural aging process. The levels of both collagen and elastin tend to diminish with age. However, it's essential to recognize that age is not the sole factor influencing this decline. In addition to age, various other factors can expedite the deterioration of these two proteins."

UV RAYS (SUN EXPOSURE)

"Solar elastosis refers to the deterioration of elastin fibers as a consequence of exposure to sunlight or other sources of UV radiation. According to the American Academy of Dermatology (AAD), sun damage ranks among the most avoidable factors contributing to the development of crepey skin. UV rays, whether stemming from the sun or tanning beds, have a detrimental impact on skin aging. Prolonged exposure to UV radiation leads to the degradation of elastin and collagen, harm to skin cells, and impedes the skin's natural regenerative capabilities. UV rays give rise to various skin issues, underscoring the importance of adopting preventative measures for the long-term preservation of healthy-looking skin."

SMOKING

"Studies have demonstrated that individuals who smoke at the age of 30 exhibit wrinkles and lines in their skin that are typically observed in non-smokers who are 40 or older. Additionally, research has identified a connection between skin elasticity and lung function among smokers. Smoking, which leads to the degradation of collagen and elastin and induces inflammation, may serve as an indicator of accelerated skin aging, potentially reflecting diminished lung function in smokers. In essence, compromised skin elasticity in smokers could serve as a significant marker of impaired lung function."

HORMONAL CHANGES

"Skin appearance can be notably affected by hormonal fluctuations. For instance, during menopause, the abrupt decline in estrogen levels results in a decrease in collagen production. Research indicates that within the initial five years following menopause, approximately 30% of collagen is lost. Certain medical conditions and medications can similarly induce rapid hormonal shifts, potentially disturbing the body's collagen balance. In the absence of adequate collagen levels, the skin tends to develop wrinkles and loses its elasticity."

How Can I Increase My Skin Elastin?

Enhancing the elastin content of your skin is achievable through various methods. Some women consider hormone replacement therapy (HRT) to counteract the decline in estrogen during menopause, which contributes to reductions in collagen and elastin, as well as bone loss and hot flashes. However, it's important to note that HRT still carries some risks, and this has led many individuals to opt out of this treatment.

Laser treatments, often administered by licensed estheticians or medical professionals, can effectively boost elasticity and stimulate collagen production. Two frequently used laser treatments, non-ablative fractional laser (NAFL) and intense pulsed light therapy, can yield noticeable results in a single session. These anti-aging skin treatments, while effective, tend to be expensive and can occasionally be associated with discomfort.

Topical skincare products have the potential to improve skin tightness and smoothness without the invasiveness of laser or hormone therapies. However, it's important to note that not all topicals are created equal. While many skincare products claim to enhance elastin and promote skin firmness, some lack effective ingredients, and even when they do, they may not penetrate deeply enough into the skin to stimulate elastin and collagen production. Given that these proteins are predominantly present in deeper skin layers beyond the surface, achieving penetration into these deeper layers is essential.

Celebrity hairstylist Ken Paves, renowned for his work with celebrities like David Beckham, cautions against daily shampooing. Paves, who has also styled stars such as Jennifer Lopez and Jessica Simpson, recommends washing your hair only two to three times a week.

Avoiding daily shampooing doesn't mean you should go too long without washing your hair. Infrequent washing can result in messy, greasy, and odorous hair, along with an excessive buildup of oils on the scalp. This surplus of scalp oils can lead to an overgrowth of naturally occurring skin yeast, resulting in dandruff.

Furthermore, the excess scalp oils can exacerbate hair loss, as these oils can trap DHT (Dihydrotestosterone) in the hair follicles of the scalp, the hormone responsible for male pattern baldness.

In general, the thickness of your hair and the amount of oil your scalp produces are good indicators of how often you should be lathering up. Here are some general guidelines you can follow:
  • - If you have fine, thin, or oily hair, consider shampooing daily or every other day.
  • - If you have medium-thick or dry hair, it's typically sufficient to wash it every 2-3 days.
  • -- If your hair is thick or curly, aim to wash it 1-2 times a week.
  • -- Regardless of your hair type, it's important for everyone to follow the proper method for towel-drying hair to prevent hair damage.

It also depends on your lifestyle.

"Frequent exercise and sweating can lead to quicker hair dirt buildup. If you use an abundance of hair products, it may also accelerate greasiness compared to using fewer products. Additionally, residing in a highly polluted environment can contribute to faster hair dirt accumulation between washes [12]. These factors might necessitate more frequent hair washing. Although your hair type plays a role, it's advisable to experiment with your hair washing frequency to discover what aligns best with your lifestyle. You might have come across the idea that washing your hair less often reduces scalp oil production, but in reality, that's a misconception. Oil production is primarily regulated by hormones and isn't significantly affected by how often you wash your hair [13]."

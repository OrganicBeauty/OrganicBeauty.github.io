Probiotics For Hair Growth

When you think of probiotics, perhaps yogurt and kimchi come to mind. You may have also noticed various probiotic supplements on store shelves, providing an excellent source of beneficial bacteria that our bodies require for optimal functioning.

What often goes unnoticed is that probiotics offer not only internal health benefits, such as addressing conditions like ulcerative colitis and symptoms of Crohn's Disease, but can also positively impact the health of your skin and hair. Topical probiotics, in particular, can help address various skin issues, hair loss, candida overgrowth, dandruff, and other concerns. Here's what you need to know about these beneficial microorganisms.

Probiotics consist of 'healthy' live bacteria, including Lactobacillus acidophilus, Bifidobacterium longum, and Saccharomyces boulardii, which you might find listed on probiotic product labels. The question arises: 'Can probiotics enhance skin health?' Probiotic skincare treatments deliver live, active bacteria that can contribute to improving your skin's well-being. This may sound unconventional, but our skin barrier acts as a gatekeeper, allowing these microbes to restore our skin's equilibrium without penetrating further than necessary. This balancing act may help alleviate issues like itchy skin and excessive dead skin cell buildup.

So, can the use of probiotics genuinely enhance skin health and combat dandruff? The answer is nuanced. Some evidence suggests that probiotics can be beneficial in reducing dandruff. For example, one study observed that dandruff improved more in a group of otherwise healthy individuals who consumed probiotics compared to a control group. However, while some essential oils have also proven effective in treating dandruff, it's essential to note that this research is still in its early stages, and more data is required before healthcare professionals may recommend probiotics as a primary solution for dandruff."

What Are the Benefits of Probiotics on Hair?

  1. "Promotes Thick and Lustrous Hair: Scientific studies have demonstrated that probiotics can enhance scalp cell count, resulting in thicker hair. Additionally, they contribute to hair's natural shine."
  2. "Balances Hormone Levels: Fluctuations in hormone levels can contribute to hair loss. Hormones like estrogen, progesterone, and thyroid hormones play vital roles in hair growth. Probiotics help maintain hormonal equilibrium, aiding in the promotion of hair growth."
  3. "Prevents Hair Loss: Probiotics combat hair loss by reducing inflammation and stress levels. They foster sustained hair follicle growth and possess anti-inflammatory properties that regulate hormone production, breaking the inflammatory cycle. Elevated stress levels often trigger hair loss, as traumatic events can shift hair follicles from the growth phase to the shedding phase. However, the introduction of probiotics can help mitigate stress and anxiety, ultimately reducing hair loss."
  4. "Facilitates Effective Digestion: Unhealthy hair can be indicative of poor digestion. Hair primarily comprises protein obtained from one's diet. However, excessive protein intake can lead to the formation of undigested toxins. When not digested properly, these toxins hinder nutrient absorption, potentially affecting hair health. Probiotics play a crucial role in regulating stomach acids, aiding in digestion, nutrient absorption, and ultimately nourishing the hair."
